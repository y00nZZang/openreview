# EFFICIENTTTS 2: VARIATIONAL END-TO-END TEXT## TO-SPEECH SYNTHESIS AND VOICE CONVERSION‚Ä®‚Ä®**Anonymous authors**‚Ä®Paper under double-blind review‚Ä®‚Ä®### ABSTRACT‚Ä®‚Ä®Text-to-speech (TTS) field is recently dominated by one-stage text-to-waveform‚Ä®models, in which the speech quality is significantly improved compared to twostage models. However, the best-performing open-sourced one-stage model, the‚Ä®VITS (Kim et al. (2021)), is not fully differentiable and suffers from relatively‚Ä®high computation costs. To address these issues, we propose EfficientTTS 2‚Ä®(EFTS2), a fully differentiable end-to-end TTS framework that is highly efficient.‚Ä®Our method adopts an adversarial training process, with a differentiable aligner‚Ä®and a hierarchical-VAE-based waveform generator. The differentiable aligner is‚Ä®built upon the EfficientTTS (Miao et al., 2021). A hybrid attention mechanism and‚Ä®a variational alignment predictor are incorporated into our network to improve‚Ä®the expressiveness of the aligner. The use of the hierarchical-VAE-based waveform generator not only alleviates the one-to-many mapping problem in waveform‚Ä®generation but also allows the model to learn hierarchical and explainable latent‚Ä®variables that control different aspects of the generated speech. We also extend‚Ä®EFTS2 to the voice conversion (VC) task and propose EFTS2-VC, an end-to-end‚Ä®VC model that allows efficient and high-quality conversion. Experimental results‚Ä®suggest that the two proposed models match their strong counterparts in speech‚Ä®quality with a faster inference speed and smaller model size.‚Ä®‚Ä®### 1 INTRODUCTION‚Ä®‚Ä®Text-to-speech (TTS) task aims at producing human-like synthetic speech signals from text inputs.‚Ä®In recent years, neural network systems have dominated the TTS field, sparked by the development of autoregressive (AR) models (Wang et al., 2017; Shen et al., 2018; Ping et al., 2018) and‚Ä®non-autoregressive (NAR) models (Miao et al., 2020; Ren et al., 2019; 2021). The conventional‚Ä®neural TTS systems cascade two separate models: an acoustic model that transforms the input text‚Ä®sequences into acoustic features (e.g. mel-spectrogram) (Wang et al., 2017; Ren et al., 2019), followed by a neural vocoder that transforms the acoustic features into audio waveforms (Valin &‚Ä®Skoglund, 2019; Yamamoto et al., 2020). Although two-stage TTS systems have demonstrated the‚Ä®capability of producing human-like speech, these systems come with several disadvantages. First‚Ä®of all, the acoustic model and the neural vocoder cannot be optimized jointly, which often hurts the‚Ä®quality of the generated speech. Moreover, the separate training pipeline not only complicates the‚Ä®training and deployment but also makes it difficult for modeling downstream tasks.‚Ä®‚Ä®Recently, in the TTS field, there is a growing interest in developing one-stage text-to-waveform‚Ä®models that can be trained without the need for mel-spectrograms (Weiss et al., 2021; Donahue et al.,‚Ä®2021; Kim et al., 2021). Among all the open-sourced text-to-waveform models, VITS (Kim et al.,‚Ä®2021) achieves the best model performance and efficiency. However, it still has some drawbacks.‚Ä®Firstly, the MAS method (Kim et al., 2020) used to learn sequence alignment in VITS is precluded‚Ä®in the standard back-propagation process, thus affecting training efficiency. Secondly, in order to‚Ä®generate a time-aligned textual representation, VITS simply repeats each hidden text representation‚Ä®by its corresponding duration. This repetition operation is non-differentiable thus hurting the quality‚Ä®of generated speech. Thirdly, VITS utilizes bijective transformations, specifically affine coupling‚Ä®layers, to compute latent representations. However, for affine coupling layers, only half of the input‚Ä®data gets updated after each transformation. Therefore, one has to stack multiple affine coupling‚Ä®layers to generate meaningful latent representations, which increases the model size and further‚Ä®reduces the model‚Äôs efficiency. A recent work NaturalSpeech (Tan et al., 2022) improves upon‚Ä®‚Ä®‚Ä®-----‚Ä®‚Ä®VITS by leveraging a learnable differentiable aligner and a bidirectional prior/posterior module.‚Ä®However, the training of the learnable differentiable aligner requires a warm-up stage, which is a‚Ä®pretraining process with the help of external aligners. Although the bidirectional prior/posterior‚Ä®module of NaturalSpeech can reduce the training and inference mismatch caused by the bijective‚Ä®flow module, it further increases the model‚Äôs computational cost of training.‚Ä®‚Ä®A recent work EfficientTTS (EFTS) (Miao et al., 2021) proposed a NAR architecture with differentiable alignment modeling that is optimized jointly with the rest of the model. In EFTS, a family of‚Ä®text-to-mel-spectrograms models and a text-to-waveform model are developed. However, the performance of the text-to-waveform model is close to but no better than two-stage models. Inspired by‚Ä®EFTS, we propose an end-to-end text-to-waveform TTS system, the EfficientTTS 2 (EFTS2), that‚Ä®overcomes the above issues of current one-stage models with competitive model performance and‚Ä®higher efficiency. The main contributions of this paper are as follows:‚Ä®‚Ä®We improve upon the alignment framework of EFTS by proposing a hybrid attention mech‚Ä®_‚Ä¢_‚Ä®anism and a variational alignment predictor, empowering the model to learn expressive latent time-aligned representation and have controllable diversity in speech rhythms. (Section‚Ä®2.2.1)‚Ä®‚Ä®We introduce a 2-layer hierarchical-VAE-based waveform generator that not only produces‚Ä®‚Ä®_‚Ä¢_‚Ä®high-quality outputs but also learns hierarchical and explainable latent variables that control‚Ä®different aspects of the generated speech. (Section 2.2.2)‚Ä®‚Ä®We develop an end-to-end adversarial TTS model, EFTS2, that is fully differentiable and‚Ä®‚Ä®_‚Ä¢_‚Ä®can be trained end-to-end. It matches the baseline VITS in naturalness and offers faster‚Ä®inference speed and a smaller model footprint. (Section 2.2)‚Ä®‚Ä®We extend EFTS2 to the voice conversion (VC) task and propose EFTS2-VC, an end-to‚Ä®_‚Ä¢_‚Ä®end VC model. The conversion performance of EFTS2-VC is comparable to a state-of-theart model (YourTTS, Casanova et al. (2022)) while obtaining significantly faster inference‚Ä®speed and much more expressive speaker-independent latent representations. (Section 2.3)‚Ä®‚Ä®### 2 METHOD‚Ä®‚Ä®Our goal is to build an ideal TTS model that enables end-to-end training and high-fidelity speech‚Ä®generation. To achieve this, we consider two major challenges in designing the model:‚Ä®‚Ä®**(i) Differentiable aligner. The TTS datasets usually consist of thousands of audio files with corre-**‚Ä®sponding text scripts that are, however, not time aligned with the audios. Many previous TTS works‚Ä®either use external aligners (Ren et al., 2019; 2021; Chen et al., 2021) or non-differentiable internal‚Ä®aligners (Kim et al., 2020; 2021; Popov et al., 2021) for alignment modeling, which complicates‚Ä®the training procedure and reduces the model‚Äôs efficiency. An ideal TTS model requires an internal‚Ä®differentiable aligner that can be optimized jointly with the rest of the network. Soft attention (Bahdanau et al., 2015) is mostly used in building an internal differentiable aligner. However, computing‚Ä®soft attention requires autoregressive decoding, which is inefficient for speech generation (Weiss‚Ä®et al., 2021). Donahue et al. proposes to use Gaussian upsampling and Dynamic Time Warping‚Ä®(DTW) for alignment learning, while training such a system is inefficient. To the best of our knowledge, EFTS is the only NAR framework that enables both differentiable alignment modeling and‚Ä®high-quality speech generation. Therefore, we integrate and extend it into the proposed models.‚Ä®‚Ä®**(ii) Generative modeling framework. The goal of a generative task, such as TTS, is to estimate**‚Ä®the probability distribution of the training data, which is usually intractable in practice. Multiple‚Ä®deep generative frameworks have been proposed to address this problem, including Auto-Regressive‚Ä®models (ARs,Bahdanau et al. (2015)), Normalizing Flows (NFs, Kingma & Dhariwal (2018)), Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPMs, Ho et al. (2020)), Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs, Goodfellow et al. (2014)) and Variational Auto-Encoders (VAEs, Kingma & Welling‚Ä®(2014)). However, AR models have linear growing generation steps; NFs use bijective transformations and often suffer from large model footprints; DDPMs require many iterations to produce‚Ä®high-quality samples. In this work, we propose to use GAN structure with a hierarchical-VAE-based‚Ä®generator, which allows efficient training and high-fidelity generation.‚Ä®‚Ä®‚Ä®-----‚Ä®‚Ä®The rest of this section is structured as follows. This is mainly because linguistic features do not contain enough necessary information (e.g. pitch and energies) for‚Ä®waveform generation. A primary idea is to use a VAE-based generator that learns the waveform‚Ä®generation from a latent variable z. This z is sampled from an informative posterior distribution‚Ä®_qœÜ(z|y) parameterized by a network with acoustic features as input. A prior estimator pŒ∏(z|xalign)_‚Ä®with xalign as input is also trained jointly. Training such a system is to minimize the reconstruction‚Ä®error between the real and predicted waveform and the KL divergence between the prior and the‚Ä®posterior distribution. However, the prior distribution contains no acoustic information while the‚Ä®learned posterior must be informative w.r.t. the acoustic information. Compare to baseline models, EFTS2 is fully differentiable and enjoys a‚Ä®smaller model size and higher model efficiency, while still allowing high-fidelity speech generation‚Ä®with controllable diversity. Moreover, we extend EFTS2 to the VC task and propose a VC model,‚Ä®EFTS2-VC, that is capable of efficient and high-quality end-to-end voice conversion.‚Ä®‚Ä®The primary goal of this work is to build a competitive TTS model that allows for end-to-end highquality speech generation. In the meantime, the proposed design choices can easily be incorporated‚Ä®into other TTS frameworks. Firstly, the proposed B2A approach could potentially be a handier‚Ä®replacement for conventional upsampling techniques in nearly all NAR TTS models, given that it‚Ä®is differentiable, informative, and computationally cheap. Secondly, the differentiable aligner may‚Ä®be a superior alternative for any external aligner or non-differentiable aligner, as it improves the‚Ä®uniformity of the model and makes the training process end-to-end. Thirdly, the 2-layer hierarchicalVAE-based waveform generator can potentially outperform the popular flow-VAE-based counterpart‚Ä®(Kim et al., 2021; Tan et al., 2022) since it is more efficient and offers more flexibility in network‚Ä®design. Lastly and most importantly, the entire architecture of EFTS2 could serve as a practical‚Ä®solution to sequence-to-sequence tasks that have the nature of monotonic alignments. We leave‚Ä®these assumptions to future work while providing our implementations as a research basis for further‚Ä®exploration.‚Ä®‚Ä®‚Ä®-----‚Ä®‚Ä®### 5 REPRODUCIBILITY STATEMENT‚Ä®‚Ä®To encourage reproducibility, we attach the code of EFTS2 and EFTS2-VC in the supplemental materials.
